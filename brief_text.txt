An  AI-Assisted  Forecasting  Utility  for  Digital  Marketing  Agencies

E-commerce  businesses  invest  across  multiple  acquisition  channels  such  as  Google  Ads,  Meta  
Ads,
 
Microsoft
 
Ads,
 
organic

Marketing  performance  is  influenced  by  seasonality,  changing  user  behavior,  inconsistent  campaign  
structures,

incomplete

Existing

are

across

This  hackathon  challenges  participants  to  build  a  practical  AI-assisted  forecasting  utility  that  predicts  
ecommerce
 
revenue
 
and
 
ROAS
 
using
 
historical
 
analytics
 
and

The  solution  should  focus  on  realistic  business  forecasting  rather  than  theoretical  modeling,  while  
clearly

Why  This  Solution  Needs  to  Be  Built  
Most  ecommerce  forecasting  today  suffers  from  three  major  problems:  1.  Many  forecasting  systems  optimize  for  traffic  or  conversions  without  considering  ROAS  
constraints.
 2.  Marketing  data  is  fragmented  and  inconsistent.  Agencies  work  with  multiple  paid  channels,  
manually
 
tagged
 
campaigns,
 
and
 
varying
 
reporting
 
structures
 
across
 
platforms.
 3.  Forecasts  rarely  explain  why  performance  is  expected  to  improve  or  decline,  making  
strategic
 
planning
 
difficult.
 
At  the  same  time,  agencies  increasingly  need  forward-looking  decision  support  instead  of  
retrospective
 
reporting.
 
Challenge  Description  
Participants  are  required  to  build  a  working  prototype  that  forecasts  future  ecommerce  performance  
using
 
historical
 
marketing
 
and
 
sales
 
data.
 
The  solution  should  generate  probabilistic  forecasts  for:  ●  expected  ecommerce  revenue  (aggregate  of  Google  Ads,  MS  Ads,  and  Meta  Ads  revenue)  ●  expected  blended  ROAS,  
1  of  5  
 ●  channel-level  /  campaign-type  /  campaign-level  revenue  contribution,  ●  channel-level  /  campaign-type  /  campaign-level  ROAS  ranges.  
The  forecasting  window  should  support  aggregate  planning  periods  between  30,  60  and  90  days.  
The  system  should  use:  ●  Google  Ads,  MS  Ads,  and  Meta  Ads  conversion  data,  ●  For  campaign  type  forecasting,  consider  seasonality  based  on  available  data  ●  Use  additional  functions  to  forecast  revenue  based  on  different  media  budgets.  
The  solution  should  support  forecasting  across  channels.  
Participants  are  expected  to  use  existing  channel-level  attribution  data  as-is.  Building  a  custom  
attribution
 
engine
 
or
 
a
 
full-scale
 
Media
 
Mix
 
Modeling
 
(MMM)
 
is
 
outside
 
the
 
scope
 
of
 
this
 
challenge
.
 
The  forecasting  system  should  also  incorporate  an  AI-assisted  causal  inference  layer  using  LLM  
APIs
 
(such
 
as
 
OpenAI,
 
Gemini,
 
Anthropic
 
or
 
similar);
 
LLMs
 
may
 
also
 
be
 
used
 
for
 
anomaly
 
interpretation,
 
forecast
 
explanation,
 
and
 
identifying
 
operational
 
risks.
 
The  focus  of  the  challenge  is  not  building  a  full  SaaS  platform,  but  creating  a  technically  sound  and  
operationally
 
realistic
 
forecasting
 
utility.
 
Constraints  
Participants  should  work  within  the  following  constraints:  ●  Forecasts  should  be  aggregate-period  forecasts  rather  than  daily  forecasts.  ●  Forecasts  should  be  probabilistic  ranges  instead  of  single  deterministic  values.  ●  Revenue  and  ROAS  are  the  primary  business  metrics  to  forecast.  ●  Existing  attribution  should  be  treated  as  the  source  of  truth.  
The  objective  is  to  build  a  realistic  and  explainable  forecasting  prototype  within  the  hackathon  
timeline.
 
Deliverables  
Participants  are  required  to  submit  the  following:  
1.  Working  Prototype  
A  functional  prototype  capable  of:  ●  ingesting  channel  level  datasets  (provided  as  .csv  file),  ●  validating  campaign  consistency,  ●  accepting  future  media  budget  inputs,  ●  generating  probabilistic  revenue  and  ROAS  forecasts,  ●  producing  channel-level  /  campaign-type  /  campaign-level  forecast  outputs,  ●  generating  AI-assisted  causal  summaries.  
2.  Technical  Documentation  
2  of  5  
 A  concise  document  explaining:  ●  forecasting  methodology,  ●  model  selection,  ●  data  preprocessing  logic,  ●  assumptions,  ●  limitations,  ●  AI  integration  strategy.  
3.  Architecture  Overview  
A  brief  overview  of:  ●  frontend  stack,  ●  backend  stack,  ●  forecasting  pipeline,  ●  LLM  integration  workflow.  
4.  Demo  Workflow  
A  short  walkthrough  or  presentation  showing:  ●  data  ingestion,  ●  forecast  generation,  ●  budget  simulation,  ●  AI-generated  business  insights.
